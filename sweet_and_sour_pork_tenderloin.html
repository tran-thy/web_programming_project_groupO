Sweet and Sour Pork Tenderloin

Who Invented Sweet and Sour Pork Tenderloin?

Legend has it that Sweet and Sour Pork Tenderloin originated in ancient China during the Tang Dynasty. A resourceful chef in the imperial palace crafted this dish, balancing sweet and tangy flavors. Marinating tender pork tenderloin in pineapple juice, vinegar, and soy sauce resulted in a harmonious blend. This culinary innovation quickly gained popularity, becoming a staple in Chinese cuisine. Today, Sweet and Sour Pork Tenderloin remains a beloved classic, appreciated for its vibrant taste and cultural significance. Whether enjoyed as a hearty meal or shared during festive occasions, this iconic dish continues to captivate taste buds worldwide.


What are the Ingredients?


  • Pork tenderloin. Choose a tenderloin with minimal fat for the best texture.
  • Green bell pepper, red bell pepper, and onion. They are the main ingredients for the sweet and sour pork tenderloin, providing rich flavors and colors.
  • Pineapple. Cut into chunks to add a sweet and tangy taste to the dish.
  • White vinegar and white sugar. Seasonings for the sweet and sour flavor profile.
  • Ketchup. Adds color and a tangy flavor.
  • Soy sauce. Adds depth of flavor to the sauce, adjust to taste.
  • Cornstarch. Used for thickening the sauce to desired consistency.
